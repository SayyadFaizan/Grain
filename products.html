PRODUCTS




WHEAT

Wheat is cultivated in various parts of the world, becoming an important part of daily diets




OATS

Oats are often considered as “healthy grains” because their germ and bran are not removed.




RICE

Rice is the quintessential staple food from Asia, highly revered as an important food source and cultural element.




CORN

First grown by Native Americans, corn is an important food source aside of rice and wheat.




BARLEY

Barley is a popular Asian grain that gains more recognition as a healthy food. Barley is used to make bread, soups, stews.




MILLET

Millet used to be eaten in Asia by poor populations, because they grow on hard soil and have a coarser texture.




RYE

Rye grows well in colder climates, where barley, rice, and wheat cannot survive.It is denser and rougher compared to wheat.




SORGHUM

Sorghum is closely related to sugarcanes and can grow on dry, harsh soil. Sorghum grains have diverse colors.
